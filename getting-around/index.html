    Getting around

    The Copenhagen city centre is quite compact, so if your legs can keep up, the best way to explore the city is on foot.

    The public transport network is well build out. We recommend using the metro, as it is quick and easy to use and runs frequently. If you prefer travelling above ground, there is an extensive bus network. The transport system is integrated, so the same ticket is valid on all modes of transport. There is a zone system determining where your ticket is valid. A 2-zone single trip ticket costing DKK 24 will allow you to travel throughout central Copenhagen for 1 hour and 15 minutes. If you need to go to the airport, you will need a 3-zone ticket costing DKK 36. Tickets can be bought with credit cards in ticket machines at major stations (including the central station and airport) - look for red machines - and at 7eleven kiosks. You can also download the app “DOT Tickets” and buy the tickets there.

    Another option is to rent an electric scooter. If you choose this option, please be aware that it is mandatory to wear a helmet and you risk a big fine if you are caught without one. There are several companies operating fleets of rental scooters. We recommend TIER as they have scooters with a compartment where you will find a helmet. When riding a scooter, you must stay on the cycle paths - not on the sidewalk.
